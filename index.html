Experience

2004

I attended Walsall College to study a GNVQ Double Award in Media and Communication. I spent two years at the Shelley Campus before moving on to university. During my time at Walsall College I was awarded the "Best Magazine Cover" award!

2006

2006 was the year I entered Birmingham City University formerly known as UCE (University of Central England). I studied for three years and during my time there, was mentored by British DJ Steve 'Smooth' Sutherland who worked for Chocie FM at the time and won an award for the "Best Radio Commercial". During my third year I was offered a job at Radio X FM, but unfortunately had to turn it down due to still being in my third year. I graduated with a 2:1 BA Hons Degree in Media and Communicatioon where I majored in Journalism and minored in Radio.

2010

I began working for various gaming sites writing reviews and game summaries, such as "Game Station", "Ultimate Gaming", and "Let's Game" before they all sadly shut down due to being unable to compete against bigger competitors.

2011

This was the year I started my gaming, anime and tech blog called Anigametec. I had a steady amount of followers and even got free journalism tickets to EGX, which was great considering I was such a small site. My blogging days came to a halt once I started a family, but it was never far from my mind.

2018

Marked the relaunch of Anigametec, but this time it was a website rather than a blog. I had a total of six volunteers and ran for two years before life caught up with me again and I eventually downsized and changed the name to "SwitchBite" which ran until 2020 as I could no longer maintain it due to work.

2022

I made the biggest career change ever and got accepted on to a Full Stack Developers Bootcamp, ran by BlackCodHer and sponsored by Goldman Sachs.
